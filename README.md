# 📊 Employee Attrition Prediction using Machine Learning

> A Machine Learning project that predicts employee attrition using the IBM HR Analytics dataset and provides actionable insights to support HR decision-making.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations as it increases recruitment costs, reduces productivity, and affects overall business performance. This project uses Machine Learning techniques to predict whether an employee is likely to leave the organization based on HR-related factors.

The project also provides valuable business insights that help HR teams understand the key reasons behind employee attrition and develop better employee retention strategies.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition patterns.
- Clean and preprocess HR data.
- Perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
- Train and compare multiple Machine Learning models.
- Identify the most important factors influencing attrition.
- Provide HR-focused business recommendations.

---

## 📂 Dataset

**Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance

**Source:** Kaggle

**Records:** 1,470 Employees

**Target Variable:** `Attrition (Yes / No)`

## 📊 Key Business Insights

- Employees working overtime have a significantly higher probability of leaving.
- Lower job satisfaction strongly increases attrition risk.
- Employees with lower monthly income are more likely to resign.
- Most resignations occur during the early years of employment.
- The Sales department experiences comparatively higher employee turnover.
- Salary alone does not explain attrition; work-life balance and job satisfaction also play major roles.

---

## 💼 HR Recommendations

- Monitor employees who frequently work overtime.
- Improve work-life balance initiatives.
- Conduct regular employee engagement and feedback sessions.
- Prioritize retention efforts in high-attrition departments.
- Use predictive analytics to identify employees at risk of leaving.
